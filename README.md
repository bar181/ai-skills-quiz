## What Is This?

The **AI Professional Maturity Framework** is a 12-level self-assessment tool that maps how deeply artificial intelligence has changed ‚Äî or hasn't changed ‚Äî the way you actually work.

AI adoption isn't a yes-or-no question. There's a wide spectrum between "I've tried ChatGPT" and "my 10-person team produces the output of 100." This framework captures every meaningful stage in between, with behavioral descriptions, psychological profiles, and organizational context at each level ‚Äî so you can locate yourself accurately, not aspirationally.

Built for **non-technical professionals** across every function: marketing, finance, operations, HR, legal, consulting, and leadership.

## What Makes This Framework Different

Most AI maturity models describe **what tools you use**. This one describes **who you are** at each stage.

Every level includes four components:

- **Description** ‚Äî the behavioral reality of operating at this level
- **The Person** ‚Äî a psychological portrait: what drives them, what's holding them back, what unlocks the next level
- **The Company** ‚Äî what the organisation around them looks like at this stage
- **The Unlock** ‚Äî the single specific shift that moves someone to the next level

The **Person** and **Company** sections are what make this framework sticky. Learners don't just read a description of behaviours ‚Äî they recognise themselves, their colleagues, and their organisation. That recognition is what drives change.

---

## Core Philosophy

Three principles run through every level of this framework:

**1. AI handles tasks. Humans handle responsibility.**
No autonomous AI for client-facing decisions. No delegating accountability to a machine. Human oversight isn't a limitation ‚Äî it's the design.

**2. Conversation beats prompting.**
AI performs best when managed like a smart intern: confirm understanding before execution, iterate through dialogue, and treat vague inputs as your problem ‚Äî not the tool's.

**3. The goal isn't to use AI more. It's to redesign what work is.**
The professionals and organisations at the top of this framework aren't more productive at the same job. They've changed the job entirely.

## How to Use This Framework

### For Individual Self-Assessment
Read each level description and locate yourself honestly ‚Äî not aspirationally. The Person section is the most accurate mirror. If it describes someone you recognise, that's your level.

### For Team Capability Discussions
Use the Company descriptions to identify where your organisation is, independently of where individuals are. A Level 5 person in a Level 2 company hits a ceiling quickly. Naming the gap is the first step to closing it.

### For Training and Workshops
The framework is structured for progressive delivery ‚Äî each level builds on the last. The Unlock line at each level works as a session hook: *"here's the one thing that moves you from where you are to the next stage."*

### For Leadership Strategy
The distribution data (above) contextualises your team. If your organisation sits at the L2‚ÄìL3 average, you're in the majority ‚Äî but the gap between L3 and L6 is where competitive advantage is currently being built.
